F-Shaped Pattern

F-Shaped pattern is a reading pattern that is prevalent amongst readers.

First scanning a vertical line down the left side of the text looking for keywords or points of interest in a paragraph’s initial sentences. When the reader finds something they like, they begin reading normally, forming horizontal lines. The end result is something that looks like the letters F or E. As shown, readers on CNN and NY Times both use the F-Pattern to read the content.

Z-Shaped Pattern

Z-Shaped pattern is usually targeted towards general audience and website that doesn't have much text elements. A z-pattern design traces the route the human eye travels when they scan a page. It follows the shape of the letter Z, as seen from the user's perspective. First, people scan from the top left to the top right, forming a horizontal line, then down and to the left side of the page, creating a diagonal line, and then back across to the right again. When viewers' eyes move in this pattern, it forms an imaginary "Z" shape.

Understanding color theory

Color theory is a set of basic rules governing how harmonious color combinations are created. The first step to establishing efficient color palettes for companies and websites is the understanding of these concepts.  To pick the best colors every time, designers use a color wheel and refer to extensive collected knowledge about human optical ability, psychology, culture and more.

"Color! What a deep and mysterious language, the language of dreams.”

The color wheel and Contrast

The color wheel refers to the disc of colors that helps us determine the types of colors. The three primary colors are red, yellow, and blue. When combined, primary colors form secondary colors. Combining secondary colors with primary colors creates tertiary colors. These are combinations of primary and secondary colors (i.e. red-purple, yellow-orange, blue-green, etc.).

If the formation and arrangement of different colors are understood on the color wheel, it'll be easier to create visually appealing color palettes. An important color theory principle concerns the use of contrast. The higher the contrast, the more two colors stand out from one another. We would want to use colors with high contrast in website design because of greater legibility.

The impact of color on our mood, emotion and conduct is referred to in color psychology. People are partly attracted to particular colors for their feeling. Colors are used to represent different kinds of feel and emotions. For example red represents passion, power, love, blue represents calm, trust and competence and so on. Typography

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable and appealing when displayed. Microsoft uses Segoe Ui font in their logo to describe their brand, while Red Bull uses Futura BQ. Both typography represents different sets of emotions and feel along with the colors used to represent.

Type face VS Fonts

Typeface refers to the specific look and feel applied to a set of alphabetic and numeric characters. Examples includes, Times New Roman, Arial, Helvetica, Courier, and Calibri.

While fonts refers to the particular instance of a typeface. Every font within a typeface has a specific weight i.e. (bold, light or normal), size of the font and other special stylizations such as italicized or non-italicized, rounded or unrounded) and so on.

Serif and Sans-Serif Fonts

A serif is a small ornamental projection off the main stroke of a letter. Fonts with this styling are called serif fonts. While Sans-Serif refers to the font without the small ornamental projection off the main stroke of a letter. The meaning of Sans in French is “without”.

Typography principles for ease of reading

  • Font should be catchy and should grab viewer’s eye.
  • Skimmability should be a major feature of the digital text as the users are looking for something and want to access it fast.
  • The font used should be accessible and readable to the majority of the audience as the website is designed to wide range of demography.
  • Fonts should be legible across any digital medium and different screen sizes. No matter the size of the screen, fonts should look universal all way around.
